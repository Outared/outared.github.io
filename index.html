About Me

Hi! I’m Sina Nazifi, a Technology Development Engineer specializing in high-performance materials, advanced coatings, and energy systems. I hold a PhD in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Houston, and currently work at Intel Corporation in semiconductor process innovation.

My work bridges materials science and engineering, focusing on scalable solutions for renewable energy, aerospace, and microelectronics. I have led R&D initiatives on multi-functional coatings, hydrogen storage, and thin film systems, collaborating across industry and academia.

My expertise includes process design, surface engineering, experimental testing (AFM, confocal, ASTM/MIL standards), statistical analysis, and cross-disciplinary project leadership.

  • Ph.D. Mechanical Engineering, University of Houston (2023)
  • M.Sc. Mechanical Engineering, University of Houston (2021)
  • B.Sc. Aerospace Engineering, Sharif University of Technology (2018)

Research Interests: Materials & Process Innovation, Functional Coatings, Hydrogen Storage, Energy Systems, Robotics, and Brain-Machine Interfaces.
